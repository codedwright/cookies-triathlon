Do I have to be an "Athlete" to participate?
Have you heard of the exclusive "Ironman Triathlon"? In about 17 hours athletes swim 2.4 miles, bike 112 miles and run 26.2 miles ! AMAZING !!! The following two videos gives a glimpse of an Ironman Triathlon.


Don't let those videos scare you, Do not feel that you must be an 'athlete' to participate. THIS event is all about encouraging one another, improving our health, setting a goal to train for, setting a personal best and most of all HAVING FUN together. Remember "Dead Last is greater than Did Finish which trumps Did Not Start!" However for those that are competitive, trophy's will be awarded in the following categories: 1-Fastest female, 2-Fastest male, 3-Fastest team

Needed Equipment
In a Triathlon you do not change clothes between activities, so wear something that can get wet and will be comfortable for all events. You will also need running shoes, a bike. If biking, a helmet is required. Fort Wayne Outfitters rents road bikes visit http://fwoutfitters.com/?page_id=38 to learn more. Not required but may be helpful, a swim cap and/or goggles, sunscreen, towels.

Safety/Liability
Everyone is encouraged to participate but obviously you know your physical health and capabilities. If you choose to participate in this event you assume ALL liability and responsibility for your decisions, health and safety. You will be required to sign a release of liability at registration the day of the race. Jeff & Lori Cook / Sponsors are not responsible or liable.

Cookout/Food
After the event is complete there will be a cookout, volleyball, swimming and fun in the sun. In the evening there will be a campfire and a fireworks show. Please bring a dish to share. Contact Rob Watson for needed items at 260-849-0139 Meat will be provided. Please choose one of the following items to bring: Chips, Dessert, Drinks, Ice, Salad, Side Dish, Buns, Fruit - Watermellon, Bananna's, Apples, Grapes, Oranges, Bottled Water. When registering please indicate what you are bringing.

Camping
Those coming from out of town are welcome to camp outside the night after the event at Jeff & Lori's.

Volunteers
If you do not wish to participate but would still enjoy helping out volunteers would be much appreciated to pass out water, ensure safety, etc. If you have an interest in photographing/videoing the event in progress it would provide great memories for the participants.

$
There is a $25 cash entry fee per participant to cover expenses. A new feature of 2013 is Race Sanctioning by USA Triathlon which will provide liability insurance for each particpant. Please bring exact amount, to be paid at registration, the day of the event. If you prepay in advance you can receive a full refund if you can't attend. All participants will receive a Logo'd T Shirt and finisher medal/ribbon to wear proudly.
NOTE-If you are already a USA Triathlon member you can save $12 off the normal $25 if you show your valid and current membership card since your membership already covers you for any USA Triathlon sanctioned events.

Training Calendar
Generally Sunday evenings are training time at Jeff & Lori Cook's. This is a great opportunity to get familiar with the course, share tips with others, learn from others, practice and just to have some fun! Contact Jeff & Lori ahead of time to confirm they will be out.

Training Tips
If this is your first Triathlon I am sure you have lots of questions.
Reach out to friends that have competed in the past for help.
For tips on a particular sport visit www.Youtube.com and search for more information.
Learn Triathlon Training Tips at www.beginnertriathlete.com www.trinewbies.com
For inspiration to improve your health, nutrition and exercise tips visit www.transformation.com and www.robbwolf.com
Use RunKeeper.com to track your training. It's a free app and can use your phones GPS so you can track your performance.
